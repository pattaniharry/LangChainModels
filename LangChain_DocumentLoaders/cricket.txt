1. Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players.
2. The game originated in England and is now popular worldwide.
3. A standard cricket field is oval shaped with a rectangular pitch.
4. Each team takes turns batting and bowling.
5. The batting team tries to score runs.
6. The bowling team tries to dismiss batters.
7. A match is divided into overs.
8. One over consists of six legal deliveries.
9. The main formats are Test, ODI, and T20.
10. Test matches can last up to five days.
11. One Day Internationals are limited to fifty overs per side.
12. T20 matches have twenty overs per team.
13. A batsman scores runs by hitting the ball and running between wickets.
14. Boundaries give automatic runs.
15. Four runs are awarded if the ball reaches the rope after bouncing.
16. Six runs are awarded if the ball clears the rope on the full.
17. A bowler can dismiss a batsman in many ways.
18. Common dismissals include bowled, caught, and LBW.
19. Fielders help stop runs and take catches.
20. The wicketkeeper stands behind the stumps.
21. The captain makes strategic decisions on the field.
22. Umpires enforce the rules of the game.
23. The ICC governs international cricket.
24. Major tournaments attract millions of viewers.
25. Pitch conditions affect batting and bowling.
26. Swing bowling depends on air movement.
27. Spin bowling relies on surface grip.
28. Fast bowlers focus on speed and bounce.
29. All-rounders contribute with both bat and ball.
30. Opening batsmen face the new ball.
31. Middle-order batsmen stabilize the innings.
32. Finishers score quickly at the end.
33. Powerplays restrict field placements.
34. Partnerships are important for building totals.
35. Defensive shots protect the wicket.
36. Aggressive shots increase scoring rate.
37. Fitness plays a key role in modern cricket.
38. Technology assists umpiring decisions.
39. Data analytics is used for match strategy.
40. Cricket combines skill, patience, and teamwork.
